# Distribution & Maintenance Policy

This document explains what to expect if this project changes distribution model.

- License: All **published releases** on the public repository and VS Code Marketplace are distributed under the MIT license.
- Future private distribution: The maintainer may choose to publish future versions privately or offer paid/enterprise builds. When this happens the maintainer will:
  - Provide notice of the change in `README.md` and `CHANGELOG.md` at least one release cycle in advance.
  - Publish a migration/upgrade plan for users who rely on the open-source release.
  - Keep a final public release available under MIT license for historical/compatibility purposes unless otherwise specified.

If you rely on a particular public behavior or API, open an issue to request compatibility guarantees or to discuss migration options.

## Support & Maintenance

Explorer Dates follows a predictable support cadence to ensure reliability and clarity for users.

### 🛠 Supported Versions

- **Latest patch release in each minor series (e.g., 1.3.x)** is maintained for **90 days** from the release date.
- Bug fixes and critical patches will be backported to supported branches during this window.
- After 90 days, the branch enters community-maintenance mode (no guaranteed fixes).

### 📣 Reporting Issues

- Users of the free core edition may file issues as usual.
- Responses to issues will be **best-effort** within the 90-day maintenance window.
- Users of **Explorer Dates Pro** receive prioritized responses.

### 📦 Pro and Priority Support

- Explorer Dates Pro is a paid companion providing advanced features and priority support.
- Pro users receive expedited responses and may request small customizations on a case-by-case basis.
